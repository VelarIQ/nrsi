# NRSI Business Model (Day-One Commercialization)

This repository is open-source at the code layer and commercial at the service
layer from day one.

The model is aligned to company financing milestones, including:

- paid pilots in year one,
- $1M+ ARR run-rate target by Dec 2026 (middle case),
- Series A readiness in Q1-Q2 2027.

## Open-core split

Free/open:

- core runtime/tooling source under Apache-2.0,
- community docs and contribution workflows,
- public issue tracking and non-SLA support channels.

Paid/commercial:

- production support SLAs,
- managed deployment and operations,
- compliance and hardening packages (SOC 2, regulated environments),
- enterprise integration and forward-deployed engineering.

## Packaging and pricing framework

Pricing below is the public framework for GTM conversations; final quotes are
contract-driven.

| Tier | Target user | Commercial terms | Included capabilities |
|------|-------------|------------------|-----------------------|
| Community | builders, researchers | Free | OSS code, docs, community support |
| Team | startups, internal platform teams | Subscription (annual) | Priority support, upgrade guidance, architecture reviews |
| Enterprise Pilot | sovereign/regulated org pilots | $200K-$400K ACV | Dedicated onboarding, production deployment, security/compliance workstream |
| Enterprise Production | scaled enterprise accounts | Contracted annual platform + services | SLA-backed support, deployment governance, reliability SLOs, dedicated TAM/FDE |

## Revenue engine

1. Land: paid enterprise pilots with measurable production outcomes.
2. Expand: convert pilots to production contracts and multi-team deployment.
3. Scale: add higher-margin recurring support/operations layers on top of OSS.

## KPI gates (aligned to financing plan)

Near-term (pre-seed execution window):

- 3-5 paid pilots,
- $600K-$1.2M ARR contribution from pilot contracts,
- SOC 2 Type I completion,
- first production deployments in named accounts.

Series A readiness gate:

- $1M+ ARR run-rate by Dec 2026 (middle case),
- clear conversion path from pilot to production revenue,
- predictable gross-margin profile in commercial services.

## Commercial boundaries

- Open-source rights do not grant trademark, certification, or official support
  rights.
- "Official" production-grade support and certification are commercial services.
- Security disclosures follow `SECURITY.md`; enterprise incident response is
  contract/SLA governed.
